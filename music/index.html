Sheet Music Library

Over quarantine, I found myself needing a large project to commit myself to help fill up the seemingly endless downtime. As a result, I took up the hobby of arranging various jazz standards for piano and then teaching myself them. When I was arranging these pieces, I tried to capture the various moods given by the pieces and stay faithful to creating the themes and atmospheres of these pieces. In the following list, I'm planning on detailing the research I did for these pieces. Hope you enjoy!

Musical Research

  • Alice In Wonderland
    • Originally the theme song written by Sammy Fain for the Disney movie in 1951
    • The song has since been covered by several jazz artists including:
      • Oscar Peterson
      • Bill Evans
      • Dave Brubeck
    • Personally I studied Bill Evans version from the 1961 album Sunday at the Village Vanguard, which in my opinion showcases the pristine harmony from the songs very basic reliance on 2-5-1 chord progresssions
  • I Hear a Rhapsody
    • Orginally a pop song written in 1941
    • It has been covered by several jazz icons, such as:
      • Frank Sinatra
      • John Coltrane
      • Bill Evans and Jim Hall
    • I personally arranged this piece keeping in mind that every voice being played, both the melody and harmony lines, should have a constant melodic movement as the song is centered around hearing music
  • La Vie En Rose
    • Orginally an incredibly popular french pop song released in 1945
    • It was the signature song of French singer Ã‰dith Piaf
    • Has since been covered by many remarkable artists, including:
      • Bing Crosby
      • Dean Martin
      • Louis Armstrong
    • I studied the Louis Armstrong version and attempted to capture a feeling of constantly falling to compliment the lilting melody and thematic ideas of falling into love
  • Misty
    • This standard was first written in 1954 by pianist Erroll Garner
    • Later lyrics were added to this instramental piece and was covered by several artists, including:
      • Johnny Mathis
      • Ella Fitzgerald
      • Frank Sinatra
    • I studied the Ella Fitzgerald version and attempted to give use chords and textures that evoked an impressionist feeling to the piece to emphaize a feeling of mist and rain.
  • Polkadots and Moonbeams
    • This standard published in 1940 and became Frank Sinatra's first hit
    • This song would later go on to be covered by many other significant arists, such as:
      • Bill Evans
      • Sarah Vaughan
      • Wes Montgomery
    • I studied Wes Montgomery's version and focuses on creating a delicate, sparkling texture, given by that of the title of moonbeams
  • Skating in Central Park
    • Originally written by John Lewis for the 1959 movie Odds Against Tomorrow
    • This piece has only been markably covered by Bill Evans and Jim Hall, besides the original
    • I tried to create motion in the arrangement that mirrored the feeling of skating, a feeling of push and pull to find an unsteady balance with an interior sweetness given by the melody
  • Some Day My Prince Will Come
    • This song is originally from Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs in 1937
    • It has later gone on to become a jazz standard and recorded by several notable artists, including:
      • Miles Davis
      • Chet Baker
      • Dave Brubeck
    • I studied Chet Baker's verion and attempted to create a large depth to piece and experimented with creatign a feeling of sweetness in the melody and tragedy in the accompinament to tonally reflect the piece
